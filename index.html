Asia Emits Over Half the World’s Gas Emissions
But Eastern Asia Drives Most of It

By: Arely Vasquez, Shaily Nieves Adame, and Zahra Ranjbar

When exploring the data on how industries and regions contribute to global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions from 2010 to 2023, one pattern stood out to us immediately:

Asia leads all continents across every major gas type.

2023 Emissions Hotspots on Industries by Asian Subregions

Graph interactive cue: Hover over different cells to compare which industries dominate each Asian subregion in 2023.


THEREFORE, industry-level data shows that "Total Industry and Households" is the top-emitting sector. Mitigation efforts should target these high-emission activities in Eastern Asia to maximize impact. As we suspected, Eastern Asia’s electricity/energy and manufacturing sectors light up as the most intense hotspots, directly reflecting the pattern in the Nature CO₂ hotspot study.

Takeaway

Asia dominates global greenhouse gas emissions, but Eastern Asia contributing the most within the region and “Total Industry and Households” sector drives the majority of these emissions, highlighting key targets for mitigation. Therefore, to curb global emissions, policymakers, industries, and individuals must prioritize strategies that reduce energy use and adopt cleaner practices in these high-impact areas.
